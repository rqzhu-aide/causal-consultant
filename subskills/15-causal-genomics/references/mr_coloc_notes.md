# Causal Genomics Notes

## Mendelian Randomization Core Assumptions

1. Relevance: genetic variants are associated with the exposure.
2. Independence: genetic variants are independent of confounders of exposure-outcome relation.
3. Exclusion restriction: variants affect the outcome only through the exposure.

## Common Diagnostics

- instrument strength and F-statistics;
- LD clumping and independence;
- horizontal pleiotropy checks;
- heterogeneity statistics;
- MR-Egger intercept;
- leave-one-out plots;
- Steiger directionality;
- ancestry and sample overlap checks;
- colocalization for molecular exposure and disease outcome claims.

## Interpretation Rule

MR estimates are not automatically causal. Interpretation depends on instrument validity, pleiotropy, population structure, measurement quality, and whether exposure and outcome associations plausibly share the same causal variant.
